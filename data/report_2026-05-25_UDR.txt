## SECTION 15: DATA AND AI ECONOMICS

**[Fact]** UDR's data assets include:
- Resident behavioral data: Payment history, maintenance request patterns, lease renewal behavior (~60,000 residents generating data points)
- Market pricing data: Real-time rent comps from ~135 communities in 21 markets
- Building performance data: Smart home sensor data (occupancy patterns, energy usage) from ~70% of units

**[Analysis]** UDR's AI applications are primarily in:
1. **Revenue management**: Dynamic pricing using market supply/demand signals (RealPage or equivalent)
2. **Predictive maintenance**: Smart home sensors predict HVAC/appliance failures → reduces emergency maintenance costs
3. **Lead scoring**: AI-driven lead-to-lease conversion optimization
4. **Renewal prediction**: ML models scoring renewal probability 60–90 days before lease expiration

**[Inference]** Estimated value of AI/data platform: approximately $50–100M in annual opex savings and revenue uplift (Inference; no direct disclosure from UDR). Marginal ROI of AI features vs. non-AI baseline: estimated 15–25% improvement in leasing conversion and 5–10% reduction in maintenance costs.

**Data moat mechanics**: UDR's proprietary building and resident data is durable but not uniquely exclusive. RealPage and other revenue management vendors aggregate data across 13+ million units — far more than UDR's 60,000 units — giving third-party vendors a structural data advantage over any single operator. UDR's data advantage is operational (internal efficiency), not platform (external monetization).

**Vendor/IP risk**: DOJ investigation into RealPage represents the key AI/data risk. If prohibited from using algorithmic price coordination, UDR may need to rebuild revenue management capabilities internally, at an estimated cost of $20–50M and 18–24 months of development time (Inference).
