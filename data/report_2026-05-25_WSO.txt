**[Fact]** Watsco sells primarily to licensed HVAC/R contractors (B2B), not to end consumers. The company serves approximately 135,000 active contractor customers across its network, as disclosed in investor presentations (2024 Investor Day materials).

### Customer Segmentation

| Segment | Est. % Revenue | Size Profile | Pain Point | Mission Criticality |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Residential HVAC contractors | ~55–60% | Small (1–10 technicians) | Same-day parts availability; credit terms | High (comfort/safety critical) |
| Commercial/light-commercial contractors | ~20–25% | Mid-size | Project pricing, warranty support | High (business operations) |

| OEM/export customers | ~2–3% | Variable | Bulk pricing, logistics | Moderate |

**[Analysis]** Switching costs are **moderate to high** for residential contractors because:
1. **Credit relationships:** Watsco extends ~30–60 day trade credit; switching disrupts cash-flow management.
2. **Proximity:** Contractors use the nearest branch for emergency same-day pulls; Watsco's density (~690 locations) is a structural moat.
3. **Digital platform lock-in:** Watsco's OnCall Air, CreditForComfort, and e-commerce platform create workflow dependency estimated at 2–4 hours per week per contractor in integrated operations.
4. **Brand/OEM access:** For Carrier or Daikin products, Watsco is often the exclusive or primary local distributor.

**[Inference]** Switching cost quantification: We estimate average contractor switching cost at ~$5,000–15,000 in time, credit re-establishment, and training — representing 3–8% of a small contractor's annual materials spend with WSO. This is meaningful but not prohibitive for large contractors who can self-source.

**[Fact]** Do-nothing prevalence: Given the replacement nature of ~75–80% of demand, "do-nothing" is rarely an option (broken equipment must be replaced). The procurement blocker is availability — if Watsco lacks the specific unit SKU, contractors will go elsewhere. This makes **inventory breadth and branch density** the key proof points.

**[Inference]** ARPU ceiling before churn inflects: For small residential contractors (~$50K–$150K annual spend with WSO), we estimate churn begins to accelerate when WSO pricing is consistently >8–12% above regional competitors on equipment.
