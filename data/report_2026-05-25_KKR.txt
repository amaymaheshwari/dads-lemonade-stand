## SECTION 10: PRICING POWER AND ELASTICITY TESTING

### Pricing Governance

[Analysis] Alts managers set fees in fund offering documents (Limited Partnership Agreements — LPAs) negotiated with anchor LPs. Fee structures have been under pressure industry-wide:

- Traditional PE: 2% management fee + 20% carry (2&20) is now rare for mega-funds. KKR charges ~1.5% + 20% for flagship PE
- Infrastructure: ~1.0–1.25% + 12–15% carry (lower than PE due to lower risk/return profile)
- Private credit: 0.75–1.5% management fee + 15% carry

### Pricing Pressure Evidence

[Fact] A 2023 Preqin LP survey found that 68% of institutional LPs reported negotiating fee discounts vs. fund terms (primarily management fee holiday extensions, co-investment rights as fee offsets). KKR's management fees per FPAUM have declined from ~0.95% (2018) to ~0.85% (2024) — approximately 10 bps over 6 years. [Analysis based on KKR SEC filings; industry data from Preqin LP Outlook 2024]

### Willingness-to-Pay Research

[Analysis] Top-quartile PE managers retain pricing power at 1.5% + 20%. The data shows: funds returning > 2.0× Net Multiple on Invested Capital (MOIC) face minimal LP pricing resistance on re-up. KKR's flagship PE funds have averaged ~2.4× net MOIC over last 20 years (KKR investor day 2024). This supports pricing retention.

### ARPU Ceiling

[Inference] At current fee rates, ARPU (average revenue per unit AUM) ceiling before LP churn inflects: approximately **0.95–1.00% blended** for institutional. If KKR attempts to raise blended fees above 1.0%, LP resistance increases materially. Wealth-channel fees (0.9–1.4%) offer higher ARPU but come with higher distribution costs.
