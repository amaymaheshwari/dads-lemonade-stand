## SECTION 3: CUSTOMER SEGMENTS AND JOBS

**[Fact]** ATS serves customers in four primary industry verticals. Based on ATS's public disclosures (Annual Reports FY2023, FY2024), Life Sciences represents approximately 40–45% of order backlog, with F&B ~15–20%, Transportation ~15–20%, and Consumer/Industrial ~15–20%.

### Customer Mix by Industry and Workflow

| Segment | Representative Customers (Type) | Workflow / Job-to-Be-Done | Mission-Criticality | Switching Cost |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Life Sciences | Big Pharma (top 20 global), Biotech CDMOs, Medical Device OEMs | Validated assembly lines for injectables, prefilled syringes, cell therapy; regulatory compliant automation | **Very High** — FDA-validated lines cannot be replaced without regulatory resubmission | **Very High** — 12–24 month re-validation cycle; ~USD 2–10M switching cost per line |
| Food & Beverage | Global CPG companies, co-packers | High-speed filling, packaging, palletizing, quality inspection | **High** — production uptime = revenue | **Medium** — 6–12 month replacement; established supplier networks |
| Transportation | Tier 1/2 automotive suppliers, EV OEMs | Battery module assembly, BEV powertrain, body-in-white | **High** — supply chain critical path | **Medium-High** — specialized tooling creates lock-in |
| Consumer/Industrial | Diversified manufacturers | Custom assembly, test, material handling | **Medium** | **Medium** — more commoditized |

**[Analysis]** Switching costs in Life Sciences are structurally exceptional. A pharmaceutical customer running an ATS-built, FDA-validated fill-finish line faces:
1. Regulatory resubmission / process validation (12–24 months, ~USD 1–5M)
2. Qualification of new supplier (6–12 months)
3. Production downtime risk
4. Total economic cost of switching: USD 3–15M per line, plus regulatory risk

**[Inference]** This creates an installed-base revenue moat: once ATS systems are validated and in production, customers have near-zero rational incentive to switch for aftermarket service and upgrades. This is the foundation of the aftermarket revenue growth opportunity.

### Do-Nothing Prevalence
**[Analysis]** In Life Sciences, the "do-nothing" option is limited by:
- Regulatory requirements for process validation and documentation
- Competitive pressure to reduce manufacturing cost per dose
- Biologic manufacturing complexity requiring automation
- However, in the current capex downcycle, "delay" is the dominant behavior — projects are deferred 12–24 months rather than cancelled

**[Fact]** ATS disclosed in its FY2024 Annual Report that Life Sciences order intake declined materially in H2 FY2024 and into FY2025, driven by customers deferring capital expenditure projects amid post-pandemic inventory destocking and tightening of pharma/biotech capital allocation.

### Procurement Blockers
- **Life Sciences:** CFO/CEO approval for large capex (>USD 20M); regulatory strategy sign-off; validation master plan approval
- **F&B:** Payback period requirements (typically ≤ 3 years); COO approval
- **Transportation:** Platform program approval; Tier 1 OEM qualification
